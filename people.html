Principal Investigator

Matiyas A. Bezabeh

Assistant Professor of Civil Engineering

Ph.D., The University of British Columbia (2021)
M.A.Sc., The University of British Columbia (2014)
BSc., Addis Ababa University (2011)

Areas of interest:

  • Performance-based wind design (PBWD) of tall buildings
  • Seismic collapse risk assessment of mass-timber and hybrid buildings
  • Probabilistic displacement-based design of mass-timber buildings
  • Mitigation of excessive wind-induced motions in tall mass-timber buildings
  • Nonlinear dynamic response of wind-excited tall buildings
  • Experimental techniques in wind and earthquake engineering
  • Uncertainty modeling and propagation
  • Aeroelastic instability of structures
  • Inelastic response of tall buildings with supplemental damping systems
  • Near-collapse behavior and collapse mechanism of tall buildings under earthquake and extreme wind loads

For Prospective Students

Thank you for considering McGill and possibly our group for research.
Two Ph.D. student positions are available in the research group of Assistant Professor Matiyas Bezabeh at McGill University, Montreal, Canada. The prospective Ph.D. students will work on research at the intersection of wind, seismic, and structural engineering, i.e., performance-based design of tall buildings. The positions are fully funded (including tuition and stipend), and will remain open until filled. The application deadline for admission is January 15, 2023, and the expected start date is September 1, 2023.
Qualifications required:
  • MS in Civil (Structural) or Wind Engineering
  • Strong background in using OpenSees or other finite element software
  • Programming skills in at least one of the following language:
    1. Python,
    2. C or C++,
    3. MATLAB
  • Research experience in the area of wind, seismic or structural engineering
  • Familiarity with probabilistic methods and structural reliability analysis
  • At least one scholarly publication (submitted or published)
